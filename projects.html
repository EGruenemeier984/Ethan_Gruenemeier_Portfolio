My Projects

Ever Since I was around the age of twelve I have been very interested in technology and I undertook many projects that have helped me develop knowledge that has made me the person I am today. Some of the projects that I have done in the past few years are listed to the right. My very first project that was in the technology field was creating a mobile application using MIT app inventor, which was created by student at the Massachusets Institute of Technology. I went through many tutorials to master this piece of software. After exploring MIT app inventor I took on my very first project, which was an app called Divergent Faction Scanner and Quiz. This was a very simple app that was inspired by the Divergent books/movies of which were very popular at the time. This was the first app that I published to the Google Play Store and as of now it has over 50,000 downloads! This project was truly the moment I realized that I loved to program and to create things such as mobile appplications. From here on I continue to explore more in depth the world of programming as well as coding. Just a couple of months after publishing the app I took many online courses on Raspberry Pi computer boards. These boards are very small but super capable, so one of the first projects I did with a Raspberry Pi was creating a LED lighting sequence using the programming language python. I created a sequence of LEDs by soldering them together and connected them to the Raspberry PI and wrote a simple Python script that controlled how the lights lit up depending on time.

Project Highlights

Divergent Faction Scanner App
This was an app that "Scanned" your finger and told you your faction from the Divergent Books
LED Lights Sequencing
This was a sequence of LEDs that were controlled by a python script
Motion Sensor Laughing Teddy Bear
This was another Raspberry PI project that used a mothin sensor to trigger a python script to play an audio file of a laughing noise.
Raspberry Pi Gameboy
This was a gameboy made using a raspberry pi and 3d printed parts. Emulates old NES, SNES, and etc. games.
3d Printer Upgrade
This was an upgrade of my 3d printer I did which consisted of tearing out the old motherboard and creating my own out a RAMPS 1.4 board and a arduino.
